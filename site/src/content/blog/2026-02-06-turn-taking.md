Turn-taking is the hidden UX of voicebots.

Most teams talk about *ASR accuracy* and *LLM quality*, but the user experience often hinges on a simpler classifier:

> **Voice Activity Detection (VAD)**

VAD decides:

- when the user started speaking
- when they stopped speaking

## Mistakes have asymmetric costs

Two mistakes:

1. **False trigger**: you start listening when nobody spoke.
2. **Missed speech**: you cut off the user or never detect speech.

In most voice UX, missed speech is more damaging. Users adapt by repeating themselves, raising their voice, or leaving.

So the â€œoptimalâ€ threshold depends on *your cost function*.
